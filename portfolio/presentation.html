JUAN ROMERO
Mechanical Engineering at the Georgia Institute of Technology

Mechanical Engineering Lead — NASA L'SPACE Program

What?

Led the mechanical architecture for a lunar rover optimized for cryogenic exploration in Permanently Shadowed Regions (PSRs). Scope included a high-torque percussive-rotary drill for volatile extraction and a passive thermal control system to safeguard core infrastructure.

How?

  • Owned the entire CAD lifecycle in Siemens NX to integrate chassis, suspension, and drilling subsystems
  • Embedded the custom drill mechanism and developed ISRU workflows to harvest oxygen and hydrogen
  • Strategically mapped Multi-Layer Insulation (MLI) layouts

Results

Delivered a structurally optimized rover engineered for extreme lunar terrain. The design enables closed-loop power via regenerative fuel cells to sustain operations through the lunar night and shields sensitive avionics from catastrophic freezing in 40K environments.

Mechanical Engineering at the Georgia Institute of Technology

Materials Engineering Intern — Department of Energy, Fermilab

What?

Decreased surface contaminants in superconducting materials used in particle accelerators at Fermi National Accelerator Laboratory.

How?

  • Tested multiple oxidation techniques
  • Characterized samples during the process using electron microscopy (SEM, XPS, confocal)
  • Developed MATLAB and Python pipelines to quantify nanoscale defects

Results

With the best oxidation technique, 20% of surface carbides were eliminated, theoretically improving material performance and allowing accelerator energy to be used more efficiently.

What?

How?

Results
